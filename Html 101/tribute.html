Jimi Hendrix

1942 - 1970

From noisiest bunkmate to legendary guitarist of all time

James Marshall "Jimi" Hendrix (born Johnny Allen Hendrix, November 27, 1942 – September 18, 1970) was an American rock guitarist, singer, and songwriter. Although his mainstream career lasted only four years, he is widely regarded as one of the most influential electric guitarists in the history of popular music, and one of the most celebrated musicians of the 20th century. Hendrix was the recipient of numerous music awards in his lifetime and posthumously.

Timeline

  • November 27, 1942 – Jimi Hendrix is born in Seattle, Washington.
  • 1958 – Hendrix gets his first acoustic guitar, teaching himself to play and joining local bands.
  • 1961 – Joins the U.S. Army but is discharged the next year, allowing him to pursue music full-time.
  • 1963 – Moves to Nashville and plays with a number of acts, honing his skills as a guitarist.
  • 1966 – Moves to England and forms the Jimi Hendrix Experience. The band's debut single, Hey Joe, hits the charts.
  • 1967 – Releases Are You Experienced, which becomes one of the most influential debut albums in rock history. Songs like Purple Haze and Foxy Lady make Hendrix an international star.
  • 1969 – Hendrix performs at Woodstock, where his rendition of The Star-Spangled Banner becomes iconic.
  • 1969 – Forms a new band, Band of Gypsys, exploring new musical directions.
  • 1970 – Releases Band of Gypsys, continuing to evolve musically but also facing personal struggles.
  • September 18, 1970 – Hendrix passes away at the age of 27 in London, leaving behind a legendary musical legacy.
